## Design Context

### Users
- **Primary**: Peruvian hospice nurses using the app on their personal phones to clock in/out and submit weekly timesheets. Usually one-handed, thumb-reach, often at the end of a long shift.
- **Primary device**: **Android Chrome.** iOS Safari is a required-to-work secondary, not the focal point.
- **Secondary**: Alee (owner) and admins — also on mobile — approving weeks, rejecting with reasons, editing shifts, managing the user roster.

### Jobs to be done
- **Employee**: log today's shift in under ten seconds; submit the week; respond to a rejection or edit request.
- **Admin / Owner**: review a week at a glance; approve or reject with a comment; edit a shift with a required reason; create, rename, reset-password, or deactivate a user.

### Brand Personality
- **Three words**: calm, legible, unfussy.
- **Voice**: quiet and respectful. Hospice work already carries real weight — the tool should not compete for the user's attention with the job.
- **Tone**: editorial. Slim serif display, readable serif body, monospace for numerals. Not clinical, not playful. Restrained, a little warm.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Keep**: the editorial theme (Gloock display / Vollkorn body / Azeret Mono numerals). OKLCH-based palette. The bones are right — we're fixing drift, not redesigning.
- **Fix**: visual inconsistency from weeks of iteration; cramped/too-small areas on small Android screens; clutter in modals and rows.
- **Mode**: light. Used in variable lighting, indoors and out. Dark mode not a target.
- **Anti-references**:
  - Desktop-first layouts and anything that assumes a pointer or hover.
  - Dense information dashboards — rows of metrics, stat grids, admin-tool chrome.
  - Generic SaaS card-grid / Inter / blue-button aesthetics.

### Design Principles
1. **Android mobile is the canvas.** Every decision is validated at ~360×800 first. Tap targets ≥ 44px. Body text ≥ 14px, inputs ≥ 16px (prevents iOS zoom as a free bonus). Nothing depends on hover.
2. **Less on screen, more per decision.** When a modal or row feels cramped, cut content before shrinking type. Progressive disclosure beats density. A screen that shows one thing clearly beats one that shows five things at once.
3. **Editorial, not magazine.** The serif is for calm legibility, not ornament. Reserve the display face for screen titles and brand. Body and labels stay quiet.
4. **Drift is the enemy.** One button system (`.btn` / `.btn.primary` / `.btn.reject` / `.btn.reject.confirm`). One spacing scale (`--space-*`). One type scale (`--fs-*`). New CSS adds tokens or uses existing ones — never hard-coded values.
5. **Respect the work, not the tool.** Every word, color, and animation is a choice in favor of the nurse's attention, not the app's presence. Confirmation over celebration.
